HISTORY OF EMPLOYMENT SERVICE

1945

The Employment Service came into existence in India under the stress of post second world-war demobilization. The Directorate General of Resettlement and Employment (D.G.R.&E) was set up in July 1945.

1952

The Training and Employment Services Organisation Committee (Shiva Rao Committee) was appointed to review the set-up and functions of the Employment Service.

1956

Administration was transferred to State Governments on 1st November 1956 based on committee recommendations.

1964

The "Craftsmen Training Scheme" was transferred to the Labour Department on 1st April 1964.

Present

The Directorate of Employment and Craftsmen Training, Assam (DECT) operates under the Skill, Employment and Entrepreneurship Department, Govt. of Assam with 52 Employment Exchanges across the state.

VISION & MISSION

Our Vision

To provide a platform to respective stakeholders for responsive transparent and efficient employment service in order to meet the skill needs of a dynamic society and to enhance employability of jobseekers.

Our Mission

To facilitate employment opportunities to all jobseekers in the State of Assam either through paid employment or self-employment and to provide vocational/ educational guidance and other services to the utmost satisfaction of the stakeholders.
